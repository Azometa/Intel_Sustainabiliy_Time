Intel Technology & Sustainability Timeline

This layout uses Flexbox. On larger screens it scrolls horizontally with scroll-snap. On smaller screens it stacks vertically. Each card reveals details on hover/focus.

1968

Intel Founded

The company begins its semiconductor journey—setting the stage for decades of innovation.

Early advances in semiconductor manufacturing laid the groundwork for smaller, more efficient electronics and the modern performance-per-watt mindset.

1971

First Microprocessor Era

The microprocessor makes computing more compact—unlocking new efficiency possibilities.

Integrating more logic into a smaller chip helped reduce hardware complexity while improving capability— a long-term driver of energy efficiency.

1978

8086 Architecture

Architectural leaps support scalable computing and influence efficiency trends over time.

Better architectures help systems do more work faster, and over time the industry pushes for higher output without proportional energy growth.

1993

Pentium Era PCs

Mainstream computing expands—optimization and efficiency become increasingly important.

As computers became ubiquitous, efficiency affected costs and impact at scale, pushing smarter hardware/software optimization.

2006

Performance per Watt Focus

Energy-efficient design becomes critical for laptops, servers, and data centers.

Improving performance-per-watt helps reduce power and cooling needs—especially in large-scale computing environments.

2013

Water & Waste Initiatives

Operational sustainability grows: conserving water, reducing waste, improving processes.

Manufacturing efficiency—including water stewardship and waste reduction—plays a major role in lowering overall footprint.

2020

Renewables + Computing

Scaling renewable energy supports lower-emission operations.

Renewable electricity reduces operational emissions—important for energy-intensive computing and manufacturing ecosystems.

2030

2030 Sustainability Goals

Long-range targets focus on emissions reduction and smarter, cleaner innovation.

2030-style goals often include lowering greenhouse gases, improving circular practices, and measuring progress transparently.

2040

Toward Net-Zero

Net-zero pathways depend on clean power, innovation, and accountability.

A net-zero future combines renewable energy, process innovation, supplier collaboration, and continuous improvement to reduce long-term impact.

2) Reflect on the process

The hardest part was building one timeline that scrolls horizontally on desktop but stacks vertically on mobile without breaking spacing. I solved it using Flexbox, adding overflow-x for desktop scrolling, and switching to column layout in a media query. I was surprised by how much polish scroll-snap and hover transitions added. I used AI to brainstorm layout/styling and to troubleshoot CSS issues by testing in DevTools and iterating until the UI matched the rubric.

3) Networking talking points

  • Responsive timeline: horizontal desktop, vertical mobile (Flexbox + media queries).
  • Interactive cards: details revealed on hover/focus for clean UX.
  • Scroll snap + subtle transforms for a modern feel.
  • Deployed on GitHub Pages and verified the live link in incognito.

4) Intel culture + tie to my goals

Intel’s culture of measurable sustainability goals and continuous improvement stood out. That mindset matches how I want to work in IT/software—building reliable systems, testing them, and iterating for better performance and efficiency. I’m motivated by environments that balance innovation with responsibility and long-term impact.
